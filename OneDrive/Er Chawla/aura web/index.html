Microscopic Dentistry uses a Dental Operating Microscope (DOM) during clinical procedures. The high magnification (up to 24x) allows dentists to see fine anatomical structures invisible to the naked eye √¢‚Ç¨‚Äù resulting in superior precision, accuracy, and significantly improved patient outcomes.

A microscopic root canal allows for better visualization of complex canals, detection of cracks, missed canals, and calcifications that would otherwise be missed. This results in a higher success rate, less tooth removal, and a more comfortable experience for the patient.

With modern anaesthesia and microscopic precision, most patients report little to no discomfort during the procedure. The microscope allows us to work more efficiently, significantly reducing chair time and making the experience far more comfortable.
